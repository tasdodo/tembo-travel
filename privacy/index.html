1

Introduction and Who We Are

Welcome to Tembo Travel. This Privacy Policy explains how Tembo Travel B.V., a company incorporated in the Netherlands and registered with the Dutch Chamber of Commerce (KvK), with its registered office in Amsterdam, collects, uses, stores, and shares personal data when you use our platform and services.

Tembo Travel operates a business-to-business (B2B) travel infrastructure platform that connects European tour operators with verified safari operators, lodges, and destination management companies (DMCs) across East Africa — specifically Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, and Ethiopia.

For the purposes of the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR") and applicable Dutch data protection legislation, Tembo Travel B.V. acts as the data controller for the personal data of individuals who register accounts, use our platform, or communicate with us in a business capacity.

If you are accessing our platform on behalf of a company or organisation, you confirm that you are authorised to accept this Privacy Policy on behalf of that entity, and all references to "you" include both you as an individual user and the business you represent where relevant.

We are committed to processing personal data lawfully, fairly, and transparently in accordance with GDPR. This policy sets out your rights and our obligations in plain, accessible language.

2

What Data We Collect

We collect and process personal data across the following categories. We collect only what is necessary for the stated purpose.

2.1 Account and Registration Information

When you create a Tembo Travel account or apply for membership, we collect:

  • Full name and job title
  • Professional email address and telephone number
  • Company name, registered address, and country of operation
  • Company registration number or VAT number (where applicable)
  • Account credentials (email and hashed password)
  • Billing contact details and invoice address

2.2 Usage and Platform Data

When you use the Tembo Travel platform, we automatically collect certain technical and behavioural data:

  • IP address, browser type, and operating system
  • Pages visited, features used, and session duration
  • Search queries entered on the platform (operator names, destinations, trip parameters)
  • Click-through behaviour on operator profiles and itinerary tools
  • Device identifiers and referral URLs
  • Log files and error reports

2.3 Booking and Itinerary Data

When you use our AI itinerary builder or submit booking requests through the platform, we process:

  • Trip parameters (destinations, travel dates, group size, budget range, travel style preferences)
  • Operator selections and enquiry history
  • Itinerary drafts generated by or saved within the platform
  • Booking confirmation data and transaction references
  • Client-facing itinerary documents you export through the platform (note: these may contain data about your end clients — see Section 5)

2.4 Communications

When you communicate with Tembo Travel or with operators through our platform, we collect:

  • Email correspondence with our team (hello@tembo.travel, support@tembo.travel)
  • Enquiries and messages sent through the platform's messaging or RFQ (Request for Quote) system
  • Telephone call records (where applicable and where consent has been obtained)
  • Support tickets and feedback submissions

2.5 Payment Information

We do not store full payment card numbers on our systems. Payment processing is handled by our PCI-DSS-compliant payment processor. We retain only the last four digits of a payment card, card type, and billing address for reconciliation and support purposes.

2.6 Verification Data (Supplier Members)

If you are an East African safari operator or DMC applying to join the Tembo verified directory, we additionally collect documentation required for our 14-point verification process, including business registration certificates, insurance certificates, operational licences, and references. This data is processed with your explicit consent.

3

How We Use Your Data

We use the personal data we collect for the following purposes:

3.1 Service Delivery

To provide you with access to the Tembo Travel platform and all features included in your membership tier, including operator search, the AI itinerary builder, RFQ tools, and operator profiles. Without processing your account data, we cannot deliver the contracted service.

3.2 Operator Matching and Recommendations

To suggest relevant East African operators, lodges, and DMCs based on your search inputs, saved preferences, and historical platform activity. This includes powering the AI matching logic that ranks operator results. This processing does not involve solely automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects — a human review step is always available.

3.3 Supplier Verification

To conduct and maintain the Tembo 14-point verification framework for operator members. Verification documents and criteria scores are retained to maintain directory integrity and for annual review purposes.

3.4 Billing and Account Management

To process membership subscription payments, issue invoices, manage renewals and cancellations, and handle billing disputes or chargebacks.

3.5 Customer Support and Communications

To respond to your enquiries, troubleshoot issues, and send service-critical communications such as account notifications, security alerts, and policy updates. These are not marketing communications and cannot be opted out of while your account is active.

3.6 Platform Analytics and Improvement

To understand how the platform is used in aggregate, identify bugs and performance issues, and develop new features. Where possible, we use anonymised or pseudonymised data for analytics. We use platform analytics to improve the search relevance and AI itinerary quality over time.

3.7 Marketing (with consent)

If you have opted in, we may send you newsletters, industry updates, and promotional communications about Tembo Travel features and offers. You can withdraw this consent at any time by using the unsubscribe link in any marketing email or by emailing privacy@tembo.travel.

3.8 Legal Compliance and Enforcement

To comply with applicable legal obligations, respond to lawful requests from regulatory authorities, enforce our Terms of Service, and protect the rights and safety of Tembo Travel, our users, and third parties.

Security monitoring and fraud prevention Article 6(1)(f) — legitimate interests (protecting the platform and users)

Where we rely on legitimate interests (Article 6(1)(f)), we have conducted a Legitimate Interests Assessment (LIA) and concluded that our interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms, given that our platform is B2B-only and the data subjects are professionals acting in a business capacity. You may request a copy of our LIA by contacting privacy@tembo.travel.

5

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or trade your personal data to third parties. We share data only in the limited circumstances described below, and only to the extent necessary for the stated purpose.

5.1 East African Operators on the Platform

When you send an RFQ, enquiry, or message to a verified operator through the Tembo platform, your professional contact details (name, company name, and email) and the relevant trip parameters are shared with that operator to enable them to respond to your request. Operators on the platform are bound by data processing terms that restrict their use of your data to responding to your enquiry.

5.2 Payment Processors

We share your billing information with our contracted payment processing provider (currently [Payment Processor — to be specified]) to process subscription fees and issue receipts. This provider acts as a data processor on our behalf and is bound by a Data Processing Agreement (DPA) that complies with GDPR requirements.

5.3 Cloud Infrastructure and Hosting

Our platform is hosted on infrastructure provided by cloud service providers operating in the European Economic Area (EEA). These providers act as data processors and are contracted under GDPR-compliant DPAs. We do not permit our infrastructure providers to use your data for their own purposes.

5.4 Analytics Providers

We use privacy-focused analytics tools to understand platform usage. Where we use third-party analytics (such as product analytics or error tracking tools), these are configured to minimise personal data exposure (e.g. IP anonymisation is enabled). A list of active analytics sub-processors is available on request.

5.5 Email Delivery

Transactional and notification emails are sent via a contracted email delivery provider. This provider processes only the minimum data necessary (email address and message content) to deliver the email and confirm delivery status.

5.6 Professional Advisors

We may share data with our legal, financial, or technical advisors under obligations of confidentiality where necessary for the operation of our business.

5.7 Legal and Regulatory Disclosure

We may disclose your data to law enforcement agencies, regulatory authorities, or courts where we are legally required to do so, or where necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of Tembo Travel or others. We will, where lawful, notify you before making such a disclosure.

5.8 Your End Clients' Data

If you use the Tembo platform to build itineraries for your own customers and you upload or enter personal data relating to your end clients (travellers), you do so as an independent data controller. Tembo Travel acts as a data processor in relation to that traveller data only to the extent necessary to provide the itinerary tool. You remain responsible for ensuring you have a valid legal basis for processing your end clients' data and for providing them with appropriate privacy notices. Our data processing terms for this relationship are set out in our Terms of Service.

6

International Transfers

Tembo Travel is a platform that inherently connects European operators with East African operators. This means certain categories of data may be transferred to or accessed from countries outside the European Economic Area (EEA), including Kenya, Tanzania, Uganda, Rwanda, and Ethiopia. None of these countries currently hold an adequacy decision from the European Commission.

6.1 Transfers to Verified East African Operators

When you initiate contact with an East African operator through the platform — by sending an RFQ or direct message — your professional contact details and trip enquiry data are transmitted to that operator. This is a necessary feature of the service. This transfer takes place:

  • On the basis of Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as approved by the European Commission (Commission Implementing Decision (EU) 2021/914), incorporated into our Supplier Membership Agreement with all listed operators
  • Supplemented by a Transfer Impact Assessment (TIA) covering the relevant countries

We require all operators in the directory to agree to data processing obligations that are consistent with GDPR standards before they can receive buyer enquiries.

6.2 Transfers to Infrastructure Providers

Where we use cloud services or sub-processors with data centres or support operations outside the EEA (for example, AI model inference infrastructure), we ensure that:

  • Appropriate SCCs are in place with each sub-processor
  • Data is encrypted in transit and at rest
  • Access controls limit who can access personal data
  • A TIA has been completed for each transfer

6.3 Your Right to Information

You may request a copy of the specific safeguards (including SCCs) we have put in place for any international transfer by contacting privacy@tembo.travel. We will provide this within a reasonable timeframe, subject to any legitimate confidentiality constraints.

Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as is necessary for the purposes for which it was collected, or as required by applicable law. Our retention periods are as follows:

Data Category Retention Period Basis
Account and registration data Duration of active membership + 2 years after termination Contract; legitimate interests
Billing records and invoices 7 years from transaction date Dutch statutory accounting obligation (Burgerlijk Wetboek)
Platform usage logs 12 months rolling Legitimate interests (security, debugging)
Itinerary drafts and search history Duration of active membership + 90 days Contract performance
Communications and support tickets 3 years from last interaction Legitimate interests (dispute resolution)
Supplier verification documents Duration of listing + 1 year after removal Consent; legitimate interests
Marketing consent records 3 years from last marketing interaction or consent withdrawal Legal obligation (demonstrating consent)

After the applicable retention period expires, personal data is either securely deleted or irreversibly anonymised so that it can no longer be linked to an individual. Where deletion is not immediately possible (e.g. data is stored in backup systems), we restrict active processing of that data until it can be deleted.

If you close your account before the end of a retention period, we will restrict processing of your data to storage only until the relevant retention period expires.

8

Your Rights Under GDPR

As a data subject under the GDPR, you have the following rights. All rights can be exercised by contacting us at privacy@tembo.travel. We will respond within one calendar month of receiving your request (and may extend this by two further months where requests are complex or numerous, in which case we will notify you).

Right of Access (Art. 15)

You have the right to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you and information about how it is processed. We will provide this in a structured, commonly used format.

Right to Rectification (Art. 16)

You have the right to have inaccurate personal data corrected or incomplete data completed. You can update many details directly in your account settings.

Right to Erasure (Art. 17)

You have the right to request deletion of your personal data in certain circumstances — for example, where data is no longer necessary, where you withdraw consent, or where processing is unlawful. This right is not absolute and may be limited by legal obligations (e.g. financial records we are required to retain).

Right to Restriction (Art. 18)

You have the right to request that we restrict processing of your data in certain circumstances — for example, while you contest the accuracy of data, or where you have objected to processing and we are considering your objection.

Right to Data Portability (Art. 20)

Where processing is based on your consent or a contract, and processing is carried out by automated means, you have the right to receive your personal data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format, and to transmit it to another controller.

Right to Object (Art. 21)

You have the right to object to processing based on legitimate interests, including profiling. We will cease processing unless we can demonstrate compelling legitimate grounds that override your interests, rights, and freedoms, or where processing is necessary for the establishment or defence of legal claims.

Right to Withdraw Consent (Art. 7(3))

Where processing is based on your consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. Withdrawal does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

Right to Lodge a Complaint (Art. 77)

If you believe we have not handled your data in compliance with GDPR, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Dutch Data Protection Authority (Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens, autoriteitpersoonsgegevens.nl) or the supervisory authority in your EU member state.

9

Cookies and Tracking

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies on the Tembo Travel website and platform. A cookie is a small text file stored on your device that allows our website to recognise your browser and store certain information.

9.1 Types of Cookies We Use

Strictly Necessary Cookies — These cookies are required for the platform to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are set only in response to actions you take, such as logging in, setting your preferences, or filling in forms. These cookies do not require your consent.

Functional Cookies — These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalisation, such as remembering your search preferences, your last selected destination, or your itinerary configuration. Disabling them may affect how the platform performs for you.

Analytics Cookies — We use analytics cookies to understand how users navigate the platform, which features are used most, and where performance issues occur. We use this data in aggregate to improve the product. Where we use third-party analytics tools, they are configured to minimise personal data collection (IP anonymisation enabled).

Marketing Cookies — We may use marketing cookies on our public-facing website (tembo.travel) to measure the effectiveness of our advertising campaigns. These are set only with your consent, which you can manage via our cookie consent banner.

9.2 Managing Your Cookie Preferences

When you first visit our website, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies. You can review and update your preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie preferences" link in the footer. You may also configure your browser to block cookies, though this may impair your experience of the platform. For a full list of cookies we use, visit our Cookie Policy.

9.3 Do Not Track

Some browsers support a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal. Our platform currently does not respond differently to DNT signals, as there is no universally accepted standard for how these signals should be interpreted. We rely instead on our cookie consent mechanism as the primary way for users to control tracking.

10

Security Measures

We take the security of personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, or unauthorised disclosure. Our security measures include:

  • TLS/HTTPS encryption for all data in transit between your browser and our servers
  • Encryption at rest for databases containing personal and sensitive data
  • Password hashing using industry-standard algorithms (bcrypt or equivalent); we never store plaintext passwords
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) ensuring that staff access to personal data is limited to what is necessary for their role
  • Two-factor authentication (2FA) available for all user accounts and mandatory for platform administrators
  • Regular security reviews and vulnerability assessments of our platform infrastructure
  • Data Processing Agreements in place with all third-party processors, including security obligations
  • An internal data breach response procedure with defined timelines for assessment and notification in compliance with GDPR Article 33 (72-hour reporting obligation to the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens where applicable)

While we take reasonable precautions, no system is completely immune to security risks. In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you without undue delay in accordance with our obligations under GDPR Article 34.

11

Children's Privacy

Tembo Travel is a business-to-business platform designed exclusively for professional use by travel industry companies and their authorised employees. Our services are not directed at, and are not intended to be used by, individuals under the age of 18.

We do not knowingly collect or process personal data from children. Access to the platform requires creating a business account with a verified company email address, which serves as a practical barrier to access by minors. If you believe that a minor has inadvertently registered on our platform, please contact us at privacy@tembo.travel and we will take immediate steps to delete the relevant account and any associated data.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our data processing activities, changes in law, or improvements to our privacy practices. When we make material changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page
  • Send a notification to the registered email address associated with your account if the changes are significant
  • Where required by law, seek your renewed consent before applying changes that affect consent-based processing

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Your continued use of the Tembo Travel platform after the effective date of any changes constitutes your acceptance of the revised policy (to the extent permitted by applicable law).

Previous versions of this Privacy Policy are available on request by contacting privacy@tembo.travel.
